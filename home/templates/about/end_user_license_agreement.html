End User License Agreement (EULA)

1. Acceptance of terms

By accessing or using the Self-Assessment of Organisational Research Readiness Tool (SORT) Online survey platform ("Platform"), you ("User," "you," or "your") agree to be bound by this End User License Agreement ("EULA"). If you do not agree to these terms, you must not use the Platform.

This EULA governs your use of the Platform designed to collect data from healthcare practitioners about organizational preparedness to participate in research studies, in compliance with applicable data protection laws including the UK Data Protection Act 2018, which incorporates the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR).

2. Data protection obligations

2.1 Fundamental Data Protection Principles

You acknowledge that the Platform processes personal data, including special category data (ethnic origin), and agree to:

  • Respect participant privacy at all times
  • Minimize data collection to what is strictly necessary for research purposes
  • Implement appropriate security measures to protect participant data
  • Maintain confidentiality of all survey responses and participant information

2.2 Prohibited activities—Data protection

You expressly agree NOT to:

  • Attempt to identify or re-identify survey respondents through any means, including cross-referencing responses with other data sources
  • Combine survey data with other datasets that could enable participant identification
  • Use demographic combinations in ways that could identify individuals or small groups
  • Share individual-level data with employers, managers, or other third parties without explicit consent
  • Conduct additional data collection beyond the approved survey questions
  • Ask probing or follow-up questions that could identify specific respondents
  • Screenshot, photograph, or record any survey responses or participant information
  • Access data outside your authorised role or research purposes

2.3 Data minimization requirements

You agree to:

  • Only collect data that is necessary and proportionate to the research objectives
  • Limit demographic questions to essential categories only
  • Aggregate data appropriately to prevent identification risks
  • Apply minimum cell sizes (minimum 5 respondents) for any demographic breakdowns
  • Remove or generalize any data elements that could enable identification

3. Security and access controls

3.1 Account security

You agree to:

  • Maintain secure login credentials and not share account access with unauthorised persons
  • Use strong passwords
  • Log out securely after each session
  • Report any suspected security breaches immediately
  • Only access the Platform from secure, authorised devices

3.2 Data handling requirements

You must:

  • Access data only for legitimate research purposes as specified in the research protocol
  • Not download or export data outside the UK without explicit authorization and appropriate data transfer safeguards
  • Implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect data security
  • Ensure secure data transmission when sharing authorised outputs
  • Maintain audit trails of data access and use

4. Legal compliance

You warrant that your use of the Platform will:

  • Comply with all applicable data protection laws including UK Data Protection Act 2018 and UK GDPR
  • Meet professional regulatory requirements for healthcare research (including NHS Research Ethics Committee approval where applicable)
  • Adhere to institutional policies and procedures
  • Comply with Health Research Authority (HRA) requirements where applicable
  • Respect international data transfer restrictions under the UK's data transfer regime

5. Data sharing and publication

5.1 Authorized data sharing

You may only share data:

  • In aggregate form that cannot identify individuals or small groups
  • With appropriate statistical disclosure controls applied
  • To authorised research collaborators under equivalent data protection obligations
  • Through approved data repositories with appropriate access controls

6. Incident reporting and breach response

6.1 Mandatory incident reporting

You must immediately report:

  • Any suspected data breaches or security incidents
  • Unauthorised access attempts to survey data
  • Technical vulnerabilities that could compromise data security
  • Potential identification risks discovered during analysis
  • Any misuse of the Platform by authorised users

6.2 Breach response obligations

In the event of a data protection incident, you agree to:

  • Notify the Platform administrators within 24 hours
  • Cooperate fully with incident investigation and response
  • Implement immediate containment measures to prevent further harm
  • Maintain detailed records of the incident and response actions
  • Support regulatory notifications to the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) as required by law
  • Notify relevant NHS authorities where healthcare data is involved

7. Data retention and deletion

7.1 Data retention limits

You agree to:

  • Retain data only as long as necessary for the specified research purposes
  • Comply with institutional retention policies and legal requirements
  • Securely delete data when no longer needed
  • Maintain records of data deletion activities

8.1 Platform monitoring

You acknowledge that:

  • Immediate suspension of Platform access
  • Permanent termination of user account
